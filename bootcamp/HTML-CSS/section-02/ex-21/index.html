Ordered list of Five Favourite Recipes to Least

  1. Poori Chatni

    To make the poori, combine the flour and salt in a bowl. Add water gradually and knead until a soft dough forms. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into thin circles. Heat oil in a deep fryer or pan over medium heat. Deep-fry the poori in batches until golden brown and puffy. Drain on a paper towel-lined plate.

  2. Gobi Manchurian

    Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the onions and cook until softened. Add the green chili and ginger garlic paste and cook for 1 minute. Add the red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, sugar, soy sauce, vinegar, and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 5 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened. Add the cornflour slurry and mix well. Cook for 1 minute more, or until the sauce has thickened. Add the fried cauliflower florets to the sauce and toss to coat. Serve hot with fried rice or noodles.

  3. Pollogiri Recipe

    Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the onions and cook until softened. Add the tomatoes and green chilies and cook until the tomatoes are soft and mushy. Add the turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt to taste. Mix well and cook for 1 minute more. Add the cooked chicken and leftover rice. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the rice is heated through. Serve hot with chutney or sambar.

  4. Chitranna Recipe

    Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onions and cook until softened. Add ginger, garlic, and green chilies and cook for 1 minute. Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, and garam masala and cook for 1 minute more. Add rice, lentils, salt, and water to taste. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 20 minutes, or until the rice and lentils are cooked through. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.

  5. Uppit Recipe

    Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onions and cook until softened. Add ginger, garlic, and green chilies and cook for 1 minute. Add urad dal and cook for 2 minutes, or until browned. Add semolina and cook for 1 minute, or until toasted. Add water, salt, and lemon juice to taste. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes, or until the semolina is cooked through. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.
