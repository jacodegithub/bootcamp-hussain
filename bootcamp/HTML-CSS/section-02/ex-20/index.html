Unordered list of Five Favourite Recipes

  • Runner Beans Salad

    Cook the runner beans in boiling water for 5 minutes or until tender. Drain and rinse with cold water. Chop the runner beans into bite-sized pieces. In a bowl, combine the runner beans, chopped tomatoes, chopped cucumber, and chopped red onion. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Toss to combine and serve immediately.

  • Bhindi Bhaji Recipe

    Wash and chop the bhindi (okra) into 1-inch pieces. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onions and garlic and cook until softened. Add the bhindi and cook, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes or until tender. Add tomatoes, salt, and pepper to taste. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes or until the tomatoes are soft and mushy. Add garam masala and coriander leaves, and cook for 1 minute more. Serve hot with roti or naan.

  • Biryani Recipe

    Wash and soak the rice for at least 30 minutes. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onions and cook until softened. Add garlic, ginger, and green chilies and cook for 1 minute. Add meat or vegetables and cook until browned. Add rice, salt, and spices to taste. Pour in enough water to cover the rice by 1 inch. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.

  • Chitranna Recipe

    Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onions and cook until softened. Add ginger, garlic, and green chilies and cook for 1 minute. Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, and garam masala and cook for 1 minute more. Add rice, lentils, salt, and water to taste. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 20 minutes, or until the rice and lentils are cooked through. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.

  • Uppit Recipe

    Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onions and cook until softened. Add ginger, garlic, and green chilies and cook for 1 minute. Add urad dal and cook for 2 minutes, or until browned. Add semolina and cook for 1 minute, or until toasted. Add water, salt, and lemon juice to taste. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes, or until the semolina is cooked through. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.
